Parent-Child English Camp Coordinator Toolkit
Operational Checklists, Guest Scripts, Itinerary Templates, and Service Standards

A professional reference material for international parents, children, partner agencies, and internal coordinators.
Prepared for planning, promotion, guest orientation, and service implementation.
1. Purpose of This Toolkit
This toolkit is designed for coordinators, OICs, airport reception staff, tour leaders, and parent service team members supporting international Parent-Child English Camp guests in Manila and Clark, Pampanga.
Toolkit Component | Purpose
Pre-arrival checklist | Ensures guests receive complete information before travel.
Airport reception process | Standardizes guest welcome and luggage handling.
Transfer coordination checklist | Helps prevent delays, confusion, and safety issues.
Tour and shopping guidance | Supports organized Manila and Clark guest activities.
Guest scripts | Provides professional wording for coordinators.
Safety and service standards | Protects guest experience and operational quality.

2. Pre-Arrival Communication Checklist
Send the following materials to parents before travel. The goal is to reduce uncertainty and make families feel guided before arriving in the Philippines.
Material | Purpose | Timing
Welcome letter | Introduces the camp, host team, and arrival support. | 7-14 days before arrival.
Full itinerary | Helps parents understand the daily schedule. | 7-14 days before arrival.
Packing list | Reduces missing items and travel preparation problems. | 7-10 days before arrival.
Airport arrival guide | Explains pickup area, signage, contact person, and first-day plan. | 3-5 days before arrival.
Currency exchange guide | Helps guests prepare cash and understand where to exchange. | 3-5 days before arrival.
Souvenir guide | Helps families plan what to buy before returning home. | During pre-arrival briefing.
Emergency contact list | Builds trust and improves guest safety. | 3-5 days before arrival.
Camp rules and reminders | Sets expectations for punctuality, safety, class participation, and parent-child activities. | Before departure.

3. Airport Reception Procedure
1.   Monitor the flight status before leaving for the airport.
2.   Arrive at the airport early and prepare a visible welcome sign.
3.   Confirm the guest name list and number of expected parents and children.
4.   Greet guests warmly and introduce the coordinator team.
5.   Assist with luggage and guide families to the waiting area.
6.   Conduct a quick headcount before leaving the airport.
7.   Provide bottled water and simple arrival reminders.
8.   Explain the first-day itinerary before moving to the vehicle.
9.   Coordinate with the driver and confirm the route to the next stop.
10.   Send an arrival update to the internal group after departure from the airport.
Service StandardThe camp experience begins at the airport. The arrival welcome should be organized, calm, friendly, and reassuring, especially for parents with young children.

5. Arrival-Day Decision Guide
Use this guide to decide whether to give the guests a Manila mini-tour or go directly to Clark.
Condition | Recommended Decision
Morning arrival and guests are not tired | Offer light Manila tour or SM Mall of Asia stop.
Morning arrival but young children are tired | Choose lunch/rest stop, then transfer to Clark.
Afternoon arrival | Go directly to Clark after a quick meal or comfort stop.
Evening arrival | Go directly to hotel in Clark. Move orientation to the next day.
Delayed flight | Reduce tour activities and prioritize check-in/rest.
Heavy rain or severe traffic | Avoid outdoor tour; use indoor mall stop or direct transfer.
VIP school owners or partner guests | Offer a polished but short cultural option if arrival time allows.

6. Tour Options and Use Cases
Tour Option | Best For | Service Notes
SM Mall of Asia | Most parent-child groups. | Best for food, restroom, rest, shopping, and simple Manila Bay photos.
Intramuros / Fort Santiago | Parents interested in Philippine culture. | Limit walking time and prepare water, umbrellas, and clear meeting points.
Manila Ocean Park | Younger children and indoor activity preference. | Good for families but check operating hours and ticket arrangements.
Direct to Clark | Afternoon/evening arrivals or tired families. | Best for comfort, safety, and schedule control.

7. Souvenir Shopping Support Plan
Souvenir shopping should be planned as part of the guest experience, not left until the last minute. Parents often appreciate guidance because they may not know which items are safe, meaningful, and easy to bring home.
Shopping Timing | Recommended Location | Coordinator Role
Arrival day | SM Mall of Asia, only if guests are not tired. | Keep shopping light and time-limited.
Mid-camp | SM City Clark or Kultura Filipino. | Give parents enough time to choose better souvenirs.
Final day | SM City Clark or Clark Airport shops. | Remind guests about luggage weight and customs rules.
Departure airport | Clark International Airport shops. | Use only for last-minute pasalubong.

Recommended Souvenir Categories
Category | Examples | Recommendation
Food souvenirs | Dried mangoes, banana chips, polvoron, chocolates, San Nicolas cookies. | Choose sealed and properly labeled packs.
Children-friendly items | Keychains, magnets, shirts, postcards, storybooks. | Good for classmates and family members.
Premium gifts | Pearl accessories, woven bags, local craft items. | Recommend to VIP parents or agency partners.
Pampanga delicacies | Cookies, pastries, tarts, crinkles, nuts, coco jam. | Best for guests who want a Clark/Pampanga memory.

8. Currency Exchange Support Process
1.   Ask guests if they already have Philippine peso cash for immediate expenses.
2.   Recommend exchanging only a small amount at the airport if needed.
3.   For larger exchanges, guide guests to SM City Clark, bank services, or recognized exchange providers.
4.   Remind guests to prepare their passport or valid ID if required.
5.   Ask guests to check the displayed rate before exchanging.
6.   Remind guests to count the cash before leaving the counter.
7.   Advise guests to keep the exchange receipt.
8.   Do not personally handle large amounts of guest cash unless necessary for safety or assistance, and only with transparent counting.
Coordinator Checkpoint | Why It Matters
Counter operating hours | Avoid bringing guests to a closed counter.
Accepted currencies | Not all currencies may be accepted.
Passport requirement | Some guests may need identification.
Daily transaction limits | Important for larger family exchanges.
Current rate | Rates change regularly.
Exact location inside mall | Saves time and keeps group movement smooth.

9. Guest Safety and Service Reminders
For Guests
Reminder | Reason
Keep passport safe. | Required for travel, hotel check-in, and emergency situations.
Carry small peso cash. | Useful for snacks, tips, and small purchases.
Stay with the group. | Important for safety and time management.
Drink bottled water. | Supports health and comfort.
Wear comfortable clothing. | The weather can be warm and humid.
Prepare child medicine. | Useful for fever, allergies, stomach issues, or motion sickness.
Follow baggage rules. | Avoid airport problems and delays.
Check customs rules. | Food restrictions vary by destination country.

For Coordinators
Reminder | Reason
Confirm flight status. | Avoid pickup delays and confusion.
Prepare signage. | Helps guests find the team easily.
Assign child-friendly staff. | Improves parent confidence.
Prepare an emergency kit. | Provides basic safety support.
Confirm transportation. | Prevents arrival-day delays.
Check restaurant capacity. | Avoids long waiting time with children.
Prepare a backup itinerary. | Weather and traffic may change.
Monitor child comfort. | Prevents fatigue and negative first impressions.

11. Sample Service Flow
Stage | Action | Expected Result
Before arrival | Send guest materials, confirm flights, prepare logistics, and assign staff. | Parents feel prepared and reassured.
Airport pickup | Welcome guests, check headcount, assist luggage, and explain the day plan. | Guests feel safely received.
Arrival day | Provide light tour, mall stop, meal, or direct transfer based on arrival condition. | Families avoid fatigue and confusion.
Clark check-in | Assist hotel check-in and remind guests about the next day. | Guests settle comfortably.
Camp days | Coordinate classes, parent briefings, tours, shopping, and child-friendly support. | Learning and family experience are balanced.
Final day | Support shopping, packing, consultation, ceremony, and airport transfer. | Guests leave with positive memories and clear learning value.

12. Final Coordinator Standard
Service PrincipleEvery activity should support three goals: child comfort, parent confidence, and program value. A successful Parent-Child English Camp is not only about English classes; it is about the complete family learning and travel experience.

The coordinator team should make the guest journey feel safe, organized, educational, and memorable. When parents feel supported and children feel comfortable, the camp becomes easier to promote, easier to renew, and easier to recommend to other families.
